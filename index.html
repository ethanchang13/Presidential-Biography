The New Frontier

John F. Kennedy served as the 35th President of the United States from January 1961 until his assassination in November 1963. At 43, he was the youngest person elected to the office and the first Catholic president.

Kennedy's presidency marked a pivotal era in American history, characterized by the height of the Cold War, the Civil Rights Movement, and the Space Race.
